When most people think of braces, they picture a teenager in middle school. But if you are an adult who has spent years hiding your smile in photos or covering your mouth when you laugh, you might be wondering: **"Is it too late for me to get braces?"**

The short answer is: **Absolutely not.** 

In fact, adult orthodontics is one of the fastest-growing areas in dentistry. Let's explore why more adults in Vancouver and Surrey are finally getting the straight smiles they’ve always wanted.

## Why Are More Adults Getting Braces?

Teeth can shift at any age. Even if you had braces as a teenager, failing to wear a retainer can cause your teeth to slowly move back out of alignment. Or, perhaps you never had the opportunity to get braces when you were younger. 

Here is why adults are choosing to undergo orthodontic treatment today:

1.  **Discreet Options Like Invisalign:** The invention of clear aligner therapy, such as Invisalign, changed everything for adult orthodontics. You no longer have to worry about wearing conspicuous metal brackets to work meetings or social events.
2.  **Improved Oral Health:** Straight teeth are not just about aesthetics. Crooked or crowded teeth are harder to clean, leading to a higher risk of plaque buildup, gum disease (periodontitis), and tooth decay. Correcting your bite can actually help you keep your natural teeth longer.
3.  **Preventing Jaw Pain:** A misaligned bite can put excessive strain on your jaw joints, leading to TMJ disorders, chronic headaches, and uneven wear on your tooth enamel.
4.  **Boosted Confidence:** A beautiful, straight smile can significantly boost your self-esteem and positively impact your personal and professional life.

## Differences Between Adult and Teen Orthodontics

While the biological process of moving teeth is the same at any age, there are a few key differences when treating adults:

*   **No Jaw Growth:** Because adult jaws are fully developed, structural changes to the jawbone cannot be made with braces alone (unlike in growing children). Severe skeletal discrepancies might require orthognathic (jaw) surgery in conjunction with braces.
*   **Existing Dental Work:** Adults often have previous dental work—like fillings, <a href="/dental-implants">dental implants</a>, or crowns. Orthodontic treatment must be carefully planned around these existing restorations.
*   **Gum Health:** Adults are more susceptible to periodontal (gum) disease. Healthy gums and bone are required before any orthodontic movement can safely begin. Your dentist will ensure your mouth is completely healthy before starting treatment.

## Popular Orthodontic Options for Adults

If you are an adult in Surrey considering orthodontics, your options generally include:

*   **Invisalign (Clear Aligners):** The most popular choice for adults. They are practically invisible, removable for eating and cleaning, and incredibly comfortable.
*   **Ceramic Braces:** Similar to traditional braces, but the brackets are made of tooth-colored or clear ceramic, making them much less noticeable.
*   **Traditional Metal Braces:** Still the most effective option for correcting very complex or severe bite issues.
