## What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is essentially an artificial tooth root. It consists of a small, biocompatible titanium post that is surgically placed into your jawbone. Over a few months, this titanium post fuses directly with your natural bone in a process called *osseointegration*. 

Once the implant is securely anchored, a custom-designed, natural-looking porcelain crown is attached to it, completely restoring the function and aesthetics of your smile.

## Why Choose Dental Implants Over Other Options?

While traditional dentures or <a href="/blog/dental-implants-vs-bridge">dental bridges</a> are viable options for tooth replacement, implants offer several unique advantages:

1. **Bone Preservation:** When you lose a tooth, the jawbone in that area begins to deteriorate. Implants stimulate the bone just like natural roots, preventing bone loss.
2. **Protecting Adjacent Teeth:** A dental bridge requires grinding down the healthy teeth next to the gap. Implants are standalone replacements that leave your adjacent teeth untouched.
3. **Lifelong Durability:** With excellent <a href="/dental-cleaning">dental hygiene</a>, a dental implant can last a lifetime.
4. **Natural Chewing:** Implants restore full bite strength, allowing you to eat apples, steaks, and nuts without hesitation.

## The Implant Procedure: Step-by-Step

Getting a dental implant is a journey, but our team at Canary Dental ensures you are comfortable and informed every step of the way.

1. **Initial Consultation & 3D Imaging:** We use advanced 3D CBCT scans to assess the volume and density of your jawbone.
2. **Preparatory Work (If Needed):** If your jawbone is too thin, you may require <a href="/blog/bone-grafting-dental-implants">bone grafting</a> before the implant can be placed.
3. **Surgical Placement:** The titanium implant is gently placed into your jaw under profound local anesthesia. The procedure is generally very comfortable.
4. **Healing Phase:** You will wait 3 to 6 months for the implant to fuse with the bone. We will provide a temporary tooth if the gap is visible.
5. **Final Restoration:** Once healed, we attach the final, custom-matched crown.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Are dental implants painful?
Most patients are pleasantly surprised to find that implant surgery is less painful than a <a href="/wisdom-tooth-extraction">tooth extraction</a>. The area is completely numb during the procedure, and post-operative discomfort is usually manageable with over-the-counter pain relievers.

### How much do dental implants cost in Surrey?
The cost varies depending on whether bone grafting is needed and the specific type of restoration. While they have a higher upfront cost than bridges, their longevity makes them the most cost-effective solution in the long run.

### Are implants covered by the CDCP?
Currently, the <a href="/cdcp-dentist">Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP)</a> does not cover the surgical placement of dental implants, as they are considered a premium restorative option. However, we offer flexible financing plans.
